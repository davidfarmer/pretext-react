By Proposition 3.1.4, every nonzero \(k\) does have an inverse in \({\mathbb Z}_n\) if \(k\) is relatively prime to \(n\text{.}\) Denote the set of all such nonzero elements in \({\mathbb Z}_n\) by \(U(n)\text{.}\) Then \(U(n)\) is a group called the group of units of \({\mathbb Z}_n\text{.}\) Figure 3.2.5 is a Cayley table for the group \(U(8)\text{.}\)
